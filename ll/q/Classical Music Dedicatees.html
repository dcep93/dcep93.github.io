February 7, 2023  •  LL One-Day Special: Classical Music Dedicatees

Smith's Blurb

Dedications in classical music are strange. Sometimes they’re expressions of artistic admiration for a colleague. Sometimes they’re heartfelt words to a loved one. Sometimes they’re merely transactional acknowledgments of a patron who funded the composer while they were writing the piece. And oftentimes they’re not even included in published scores.

This quiz covers a variety of classical pieces’ dedications and explores the backstories behind them. Questions are arranged roughly in order of difficulty, with easier questions in the first half and harder questions in the second half. At least some familiarity with classical music is necessary to get most of the questions, but I hope that the stories and personalities highlighted will be interesting and enjoyable regardless of whether you don't know Franz Schubert from Robert Schumann or if you read the Norton Anthology of Western Music every night before bed.
