1.  The films of Orson Welles and Martin Scorsese are quite different (to put it mildly), but the directors share a connection in that they bookended the career of what composer (most associated with Alfred Hitchcock), who scored his first film, Citizen Kane, in 1941 and died the day after completing work on Taxi Driver in 1975?

Click here to reveal answer
BERNARD HERMANN

2.  Chinese-born composer Tan Dun is best known for concert music, but he has also written scores to five films, including Don't Cry, Nanking and Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon. The score to the latter film extensively features solo passages performed by what internationally renowned cellist?

Click here to reveal answer
YO-YO MA

3.  The first woman to win an Academy Award for scoring a film did so for the 1996 movie Emma. Name the composer.

Click here to reveal answer
RACHEL PORTMAN

4.  In the early 1920s, Carl Stalling worked as an organist playing musical accompaniments to silent films at the Isis Theater in Kansas City, Missouri. One day he met a little-known young artist barely out of his teens who had particularly admired Stalling's ability to mix pre-existing musical material with improvisations. This chance meeting led to a career in which Stalling composed the scores to over 600 short films, many of them hugely successful. Who was this young artist Stalling met in Kansas City?

Click here to reveal answer
WALT DISNEY

5.  The 1955 Bengali-language film Pather Panchali introduced much of the Western world to Indian cinema, and it was a major influence on filmmakers from many countries. It was also a turning point in the career of the sitarist who wrote and performed the music (and who was the biological father of a major American pop star). Who is the sitarist/composer?

Click here to reveal answer
RAVI SHANKAR

6.  A popular — viral, even — 1995 film featured a riveting score by Paul Buckmaster whose main theme was based on the Suite Punta del Este by tango composer Astor Piazzolla. (It also featured an equally riveting on-screen turn by Brad Pitt, who earned an Oscar nomination for his performance as Jeffrey Goines.) Name the film.

Click here to reveal answer
12 MONKEYS

7.  2021's Encanto featured chart-busting songs by Lin-Manuel Miranda, but we should also talk about the film's score, which earned its composer the first best-score Oscar nomination for a Latina. What is her name?

Click here to reveal answer
GERMAINE FRANCO

8.  Stanley Kubrick's 1968 film 2001: A Space Odyssey is famous for its use of classical music, such as Richard Strauss's Also Sprach Zarathustra and Johann Strauss's Blue Danube waltz, but the film did have an original score composed for it by Alex North. Kubrick abandoned the score after it was completed, even though he had previously worked with North on what 1960 swords-and-sandals epic starring Kirk Douglas in the title role?

Click here to reveal answer
SPARTACUS

9.  The composer of the concert piece The Carnival of the Animals and an Organ Symphony was also responsible for the score to the 1908 film The Assassination of the Duke of Guise (La Mort du duc de Guise). As he was born in 1835, he is likely the earliest-born person to have composed music specifically for a movie. Who was the composer?

Click here to reveal answer
CAMILLE SAINT-SAËNS

10.  Hans Zimmer's score to Interstellar prominently features a certain solo instrument. This was at the request of directpr Christopher Nolan, who felt that this instrument represents "mankind's attempt to portray the mystical or the metaphysical — what's beyond us, beyond the realm of the everyday." What is the solo instrument?

Click here to reveal answer
PIPE ORGAN

11.  1967's In the Heat of the Night might be best known for its stellar performances by Sidney Poitier and Rod Steiger, but it also features a powerhouse score written by what music legend? Though born in Chicago, he is typically associated with Seattle, and his family members include an actress daughter whose many roles have included the character of Ann Perkins on Parks and Recreation from 2009 to 2015.

Click here to reveal answer
QUINCY JONES

12.  George Bruns got the credit for scoring a 1959 animated Disney tale, but almost all of the film's music was based on music composed by Tchaikovsky for a ballet with essentially the same name as the film. Name either the film or the ballet.

Click here to reveal answer
SLEEPING BEAUTY, THE SLEEPING BEAUTY

Smiths' Blurb

Click to view blurb
Click to hide blurb
Your smiths are a brother-sister duo with film scores in the blood. Our dad was an audio engineer who worked on film score recordings, and we’ve both been involved in the industry as well — one as a musician (along with his wife and our mom), one as a gofer, and both of us as semi-obsessed connoisseurs. Composer Howard Shore once said, “Music is essentially an emotional language,” and where would the emotion of film — and thus cinema itself — be without its music?

The questions are fairly niche, but we’ve tried to add multiple points of entry wherever possible. Here’s hoping you leave this one-day feeling as inspired by the power of film scores as we are.
