Smith's Blurb

Click to view blurb
Click to hide blurb
"Where there's a hit, there's a writ."
- Old music industry adage; source unknown

In the early morning I can't make up a thing
And I can barely play, I don't like to sing
So I picked up my brush painted blue guitar
And I ripped off the chords from "Bron-Yr-Aur"
- Red House Painters

"I don't think anybody steals anything; all of us borrow."
- B.B. King

Musicians borrowing ideas and themes from their ancestors and contemporaries is a grand tradition that extends from prehistoric times to the folk and blues traditions and into the digital era with the invention of sampling and the rise of hip-hop. Most of the time when this happens, the "lenders" either never know about it or just let it go, but sometimes lawyers get involved.

This 1DS will explore twelve cases of music plagiarism and copyright infringement, some famous, others infamous, others still relatively obscure but no less significant. Many genres and styles will be represented. Anyone with a good grounding in music trivia and/or pop culture should do well on this quiz; no knowledge of the legal system will be necessary. I'm estimating that this 1DS will land somewhere between ModKos PG-13 and R.

Save the date - the 6th of April, come she will, when streams are ripe and swelled with rain, chestnuts in blossom, holiday tables under the trees...*

* Yes, I just plagiarized Paul Simon and Yip Harburg. I hope those guys have a good sense of humor and don't take us into court. **
** I just plagiarized The Dead Milkmen. We can't stop, and we won't stop. ***
*** I'm sure Miley's lawyers have better things to do.
