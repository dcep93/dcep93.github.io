Smith's Blurb

Click to view blurb
Click to hide blurb
Welcome to the Countryside recitaLL haLL! Which country? Get a good pair of headphones, turn up your volume, and settle in, because your task is to answer that question -- "which country" -- twelve times.

Today's program features selections of about 40-60 seconds each extracted from twelve pieces of classical music written over several centuries. The formal or common TITLE of each excerpted work (possibly including the name of the specific movement you are listening to) contains AT LEAST ONE of the following:
* the name of a modern-day country in English or cognate to the English name (e.g.: Dominican Republic, República Dominicana)
* the demonym for a person from a modern-day country (e.g.: Dominican)
* the same name that a modern-day country is named after, but in a different language (e.g.: Dominique, Domingo)

The titles of the work may also contain other geographic terms, like names of regions or cities (e.g.: Quisqueyan, Hispaniola, Santiago) but those will not be considered relevant in this quiz.

Your task is to give the modern-day COUNTRY listed in the title of the work (unless otherwise noted).
If more than one country is present, you need only give one.

For an extremely contrived example, if the work were titled "Simon Bolivar leading the Dominican capture of Port-au-Prince", you would get credit for answering "Bolivia" or "Dominican Republic" or "Dominica" but not "Port-au-Prince" or "Haiti".

You'll also get to see the identities of the artists who perform in each of the recordings you hear (taken from my digital music library). I list them so as to give the artists proper credit; you should not be swayed to or from a specific answer based on the nationalities of the soloists or conductors or by the locations of the orchestras featured in these excerpts.

The lights are dimming, the audience begins to clap, and ... here's Artur Rubinstein walking across the stage, sitting down at the piano, and he begins to play ....
