Smith's Blurb

Click to view blurb
Click to hide blurb
Don’t you hate it when you request that the DJ play Pink Floyd’s “Wish You Were Here” but they end up playing that song by Incubus, which is not at all a cover of the Pink Floyd song?

Or when you request “Thank You,” but then realize that could refer to the song from Led Zeppelin’s album II or the unrelated one from Boyz II Men’s album II—and you really meant the hit by Dido?

How about “Somebody to Love?” Are you going to get the song by Queen? Jefferson Airplane? Justin Bieber? Nelly Furtado? OneRepublic?

Clearly this is a problem that plagues you frequently, so we should go over some of these ambiguities. This One-Day Special is about songs that share titles despite the fact that one is not a cover of the other. The questions relate to songs with fairly simple one-word titles (such as Patsy Cline's "Crazy" and Gnarls Barkley's "Crazy") as well as song titles that are so specific that it's hard to imagine the later artist wasn't referencing the previous one (such as when the Black Keys released a song in 2010 called "Never Gonna Give You Up" which was in no way a cover of the Rick Astley song).
