Smith's Blurb

Click to view blurb
Click to hide blurb
Welcome!! This 1DS accesses "deep cut" lyrics within 12 songs – from a diversity of decades, genres, and artists – that are widely known and/or may be characterized as "classics."

These designations are of course subjective and relative to particular contexts, so in my selection of the songs I have used resources like Billboard hit lists to try to be as objective as possible. In this case, "widely known" and “classic” means that more than half of them hit #1 on the weekly Billboard Hot 100 chart, and all twelve songs peaked in the top ten (but the hope is that you’d recognize at least part of all of them). The songs all come from the period 1969-2019. Five are by female artists; seven are by male artists. Six are by POC/non-white artists; six are by white artists. And the vast majority of them were produced in the United States. (Please just use that as a hint and not a suggestion that America is #1. It’s most decidedly not.)

Within these commonly recognized songs, the selected lyrics are generally lesser-known for a variety of reasons. For example, maybe they are in a “weird” part of the song like a bridge or verse that your brain sort of ignores while waiting for the chorus to sing along. Maybe they aren’t pronounced super clearly and you can’t figure out what they’re actually saying. Maybe you’ve only heard one part of the song over & over (like in a movie or commercial) and don’t have any inkling of the rest of the words. Today we’re interested in those weird lyrics that are lurking in these familiar tunes, the ones that you see at karaoke and you're like "wait, what??"

PLEASE READ THIS PART SO YOU KNOW HOW TO ANSWER:

The questions consist of the lyrics and the year the song was released.

Your job is to provide the SONG TITLE.

The artist will be provided in the answer, but this is just for your information. YOU SHOULD NOT PUT THE ARTIST IN YOUR ANSWER.
