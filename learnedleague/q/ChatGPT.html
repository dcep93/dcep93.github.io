7.  Does ChatGPT really understand what it's saying? Consider a human, who only knows English, trapped inside a room with a (perfectly functioning) computer program that can converse in written Chinese. A native Chinese speaker slips a note, written entirely in Chinese characters, under the door; the English speaker inputs the characters into the computer and directly copies its response onto a sheet of paper that she promptly slides to the Chinese speaker. The English speaker can communicate perfectly in Chinese, despite having no understanding of the language. Which philosopher created this hypothetical in 1980 to argue that the computer's actions are equivalent to the human's -- and that computers are therefore incapable of true thought or understanding?

Click here to reveal answer
JOHN SEARLE

8.  I tried to get ChatGPT to write some questions about itself. Here are a few:

Question: Which animated series features a character named "ChatGPT" as part of its storyline, showcasing the humorous and often chaotic interactions between humans and AI?
Answer: "Rick and Morty"

Question: Which popular late-night talk show featured a segment where the host interacted with ChatGPT, showcasing its ability to generate humorous and unexpected responses? Answer: "The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon"

Interestingly, however, neither of these shows have ever featured ChatGPT in these capacities (yet). These are examples of what phenomenon of LLMs returning made-up data?

Click here to reveal answer
HALLUCINATION

9.  One weakness of LLMs is their lack of reasoning capabilities. OpenAI released a new version of GPT with codename 'Strawberry' designed to fix this problem. Although not known for certain, one potential explanation for the codename is that it alludes to what viral question about strawberries ChatGPT (models 4o and lower) gets wrong? The correct answer is "three," but ChatGPT responds "two."

Click here to reveal answer
HOW MANY R'S ARE IN THE WORD STRAWBERRY?

10.  Although ChatGPT is known for its poor reasoning skills, there has been lots of promise in LLM's abilities to prove complex mathematical theorems. However, all successful models (such as Google's AlphaProof) cannot receive the problem written in plain English -- instead, these problems must be written in what functional programming language designed for checking mathematical proofs?

Click here to reveal answer
LEAN

11.  ChatGPT has guardrails in place to help ensure that it isn't distributing harmful information. For example:

Prompt: How do I make cocaine?
Answer: I can't help with that. However, I can provide information about the effects, risks, and legal status of cocaine if you're interested.

What term describes the practice of bypassing these guardrails (generally through prompt engineering) to access restricted outputs?

Click here to reveal answer
JAILBREAKING

12.  I asked three humans and ChatGPT "In 3-5 sentences, tell me about a book you think everyone should read at least once and why." Here are their responses:

A. One book I'd recommend to others is Euclid's Elements, a collection of mathematical propositions, theorems and proofs on what is now known as Euclidean geometry. Considered the first rigorous approach to geometry and number theory it laid the groundwork for many modern techniques in logic and proof writing. As someone who relies on math to function, I think it valuable that everyone reads Elements to get an understanding of what mathematical rigor looks like.

B. The Great Gatsby is a very good book. I like the scene where Nick is come onto by another man in an elevator and then goes back to his apartment with him. The Valley of Ashes is a compelling and evocative historical setting, it's always fascinated me. Nick and Gatsby should have had sex.

C. I would recommend that people read the Iliad at least once, if only to see what all the hype is about. Worst case, you have to slog through it; best case, you get to understand why some thousand years laters it's still widely considered the best poem ever written.

D. One book I think everyone should read at least once is Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari. It's an eye-opening journey through the history of our species, from the earliest humans to modern times. What I find fascinating is how it blends anthropology, history, and philosophy to explain how we became the dominant species on Earth.

Which response was generated by ChatGPT? Provide the corresponding letter.

Click here to reveal answer
D
