1.  The [BLANK] spills the soup, the [BLANK] has the soup land on them, and the nudnik asks "So, what kind of soup was it?" In order, what two alliterative Yiddish words fill in the blanks? Shirley you know they refer, respectively, to a clumsy bungler and a persistently unlucky person.

Click here to reveal answer
SCHLEMIEL, SCHLIMAZEL

2.  "Tragedy is when I cut my finger. Comedy is when you fall into an open sewer and die." Name the comedian who, at 98 years young, only has 1,902 years to go before he becomes one of his famous characters.

Click here to reveal answer
MEL BROOKS

3.  They tried to capture moonlight in a barrel of water. They built a roof over a sundial to keep it from getting wet. They declared the moon to be more important than the sun, for the sun shines during the day when it is already bright out, but the moon shines at night when it is dark. They hung the charity box in their synagogue from the ceiling so it could not be stolen, and then installed a ladder so that donations could be made to it. They are, of course, the fictional "Wise Men" of a real Polish town. Name the town.

Click here to reveal answer
CHELM

4.  Of the nine original cast members of Saturday Night Live, three were Jewish. One of them, George Coe, was only billed in the pilot, though he had several small roles throughout the season. The other two portrayed famous characters such as Baba Wawa, Roseanne Roseannadanna, Emily Litella, Connie Conehead, and Christie Christina; name either cast member.

Click here to reveal answer
GILDA RADNER, LARAINE NEWMAN

5.  "I know, I know. We are the chosen people. But, once in a while, can't You choose someone else?" This is a line uttered by Tevye the Milkman in the 1971 film Fiddler on the Roof. Name the Yiddish author and playwright whose short stories about Tevye form the basis for the movie.

Click here to reveal answer
SHOLEM ALEICHEM

6.  Below is a dialogue between Marx brothers Groucho and Chico, taken from the 1935 film A Night at the Opera. We've lightly redacted the script and corresponding audio clip; your job is to fill in the exact two-word phrase which below is replaced with "[BLANK]" twice.

Groucho: Just you put your name right down there and then the deal is legal.
Chico: I forgot to tell you, I can't write.
Groucho: That's alright, there's no ink in the pen anyhow. But listen, it's a contract, isn't it?
Chico: Oh sure.
Groucho: We got a contract, no matter how small it is.
Chico: Hey wait, wait. What does this say here, this thing here?
Groucho: Oh that? Oh that's the usual [redacted]. That's in every contract. That just says if any of the parties participating in this contract is shown not to be in their right mind, the entire agreement is automatically nullified.
Chico: Well, I don't know.
Groucho: It's alright! That's in every contract. That's what they call a [BLANK].
Chico: (Laughing.) You can't fool me! There ain't no [BLANK]!

7.  This comedian learned of her Jewish heritage at the age of 27, when she reconnected with her Jewish Eritrean father. 13 years later, she celebrated her bat mitzvah on the same day as the release of her 2019 Netflix special, Black Mitzvah, the album version of which would earn her a Grammy for best comedy album. Name this comedian, whose last name can be obtained by substituting one letter of a Jewish prayer (likely recited during her bat mitzvah).

Click here to reveal answer
TIFFANY HADDISH

8.  One classic Jewish joke tells the story of two women at a Catskill Mountains resort. One says to the other "The food here is awful." The other responds "I know. And such small portions!"

In the mid-twentieth century, a large number of Jews from downstate New York would spend their summers in the Catskills, at resorts like the one mentioned above (though hopefully with better food). Performing at these resorts were a variety of famous Jewish comedians, ranging from Lenny Bruce to Rodney Dangerfield. Give the alliterative two-word name for the geographic region home to these resorts and entertainment circuit, purportedly coined by Variety editor Abel Green.

Click here to reveal answer
BORSCHT BELT

9.  Famous for her renditions of such songs as "I Ain't Takin' Orders from No One," "I Don't Want to Get Thin," and "My Yiddishe Momme" (the lattermost banned in Nazi Germany), what raunchy and comedic vaudeville performer of the early 20th century was nicknamed "the last of the Red Hot Mamas"? She is said to have influenced the acts of Mae West, Joan Rivers, Ethel Merman, and Bette Midler, among others.

Click here to reveal answer
SOPHIE TUCKER

10.  "Want to hear a riddle? What's purple, hangs on a wall, and whistles?"
"I don't know. I give up."
"A [BLANK]."
"A [BLANK] isn't purple!"
"It is if you paint it purple."
"A [BLANK] doesn't hang on a wall!"
"It does if you hang it there."
"A [BLANK] doesn't whistle!"
"Oh, I just threw that in to make it harder."

What word, a tasty animal of the genus Clupea, fills in the blanks, completing this old Jewish joke?

Click here to reveal answer
HERRING

11.  "Of course this is a good violin – it's a Stradivarius. It better be or I'm out 60 bucks." This joke (with minor variations) is actually attributed to two Jewish violinist comedians. One wrote an autobiography entitled Take My Life, Please, a play on perhaps his most famous joke. The other's autobiography was unfinished when he died at the age of 80, despite his perpetual insistence that he was really 39. Name either comedian. (Please name only one comedian, allowing us to wonder why you didn't like the other one.)

Click here to reveal answer
HENNY YOUNGMAN, JACK BENNY

12.  If you were hoping this 1DS would conclude without one last Jewish joke, we're afraid not. A rabbi spills red wine on his favorite tallit (a Jewish prayer shawl) and brings it to the dry cleaners to have the stain removed. When he sees the final bill, he cries out "$500?! Just to remove a stain?" The dry cleaner replies, "Oh, removing the stain was easy, but getting out all those little knots was a nightmare!" What is the Hebrew name of the knotted fringes the dry cleaner is referring to, without which a tallit is no longer considered kosher?

Click here to reveal answer
TZITZIT
