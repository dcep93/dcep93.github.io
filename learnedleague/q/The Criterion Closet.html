Matthew Modine

Stanley said that he wanted to make a movie that was a serious movie about the Doomsday machine, and about nuclear proliferation, and nuclear warfare, and the terrifying aspects of it. And the more he did research, the more he realized how stupid the entire thing was and that you couldn't make a serious movie about this because the absurdity of human behavior and the industrial military complex — it was so ridiculous that the only way you could make a really good movie about the nuclear war and nuclear bombs was to make a comedy.

5.  Nathan Lane

I sort of retired from musicals, but I went through a period where I thought if I was gonna do a musical, I would like to do a musical of [REDACTED]. Because it would be an interesting challenge… I asked Sondheim if he would be interested in doing it, and he said "But the central character is a cypher, he doesn't sing." And I said, "I know, but… it forces other people to sing at him." I said, "It's an interesting dynamic, don't you think?" I just thought, for one thing, with a character like this, I wouldn't have to sing for a while. I wouldn't sing until the end of the first Act when the President of the United States would turn to me and say, "What do you think Mr. Gardiner?" And Chauncey says, "Well, it's like a garden." Ding! And you'd hear the musical cue, and then he would sing this childlike song about gardening and yet the President would see like it was a great analogy for the country, and then other people would start singing it, it would be on the news, and then children in schools would be singing this song and then it would build to this huge, almost frightening anthem that would end the first Act. That was my concept.

6.  Gael García Bernal

I'm glad that they never changed the title into English because it doesn't sound so good. Smith's Blurb

Click to view blurb +
Click to hide blurb –
Ah, the Criterion Closet. The ultimate mecca for movie nerds. The place where cinephiles lay prostrate at the feet of the masters.

What's that, you say? You don't know what the Criterion Closet is? No need to fear. If you like movies, this One-Day is for you! For the uninitiated, the Criterion Collection periodically invites filmmakers into its famed Criterion Closet, a room full of blu-rays and DVDs of movies in the collection. Criterion asks its invitees to pick out their favorite films and to talk about them. This series is available on the Criterion Channel and posted on YouTube.

This One-Day is simple. I will provide (i) a quote given by a Criterion Closet guest about one of the movies that he or she has chosen; and (ii) the name of the guest. You have to guess the title of the movie based on that information alone.

Enough questions should be accessible for the casual movie fan, but I expect the true cinephiles to shine.

Please note that I have slightly adapted the quotes for readability or other purposes.
