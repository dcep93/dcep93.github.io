3.  Aerosmith has had several successful albums, but fans felt stuck in 1982 when what poorly received album came out? It is also the only Aerosmith album not to feature Joe Perry.

4.  Passage, a creative departure released in 1977, was retroactively described by a critic as "admirable even if most of the results aren't memorable or essential." Which often bubbly, accessible vocal and instrumental sibling duo released this album that memorably featured a science-fiction themed cover song?

5.  Lauryn Hill, despite being one of the most acclaimed and influential performers of her time, only has two full length solo albums to her name. One, of course, is the incomparable The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill. The other is a live album that was met with mixed reviews, and was recorded in what notable way?

Click here to reveal answer
UNPLUGGED

6.  Equator, Raging Silence, and Fallen Angel are three of the many just okay albums from this wizardly progressive hard rock band's very prolific discography. Their first few albums were quite successful, and back then, it was easy livin'. Who is this band, who have released music with relative consistency since their inception, including 12 albums in the 70s alone!

Click here to reveal answer
URIAH HEEP

7.  Katy Perry decidedly lost a diss track battle when she showcased her bad blood with which singer in "Swish Swish," a single on the critically unacclaimed album Witness? The album's heavy promotion, eponymous tour, and cringey performance with Migos on SNL did not make Perry look any better.

Click here to reveal answer
TAYLOR SWIFT

8.  Bob Dylan is a beloved counter-cultural icon. Therefore, many of his fans were confounded by his sudden shift to the establishment in 1979-81, when he released a Christian trilogy! After these commercial disasters, Dylan found redemption and forgiveness by returning to non-Christian themes. Name any one of the albums in this trilogy.

Click here to reveal answer
SLOW TRAIN COMING, SAVED, OR SHOT OF LOVE

9.  In 1999, this R & B group released Finally, three years after a smash hit album containing one of the 90s most enduring singles. While Finally did receive critical acclaim, it was not commercially successful. There was no doubt, neither diggity, that the group was then dropped from Interscope Records. Who are they?

Click here to reveal answer
BLACKSTREET

10.  Elaborate concept albums are always a gamble. Which 1993 Billy Idol album, called pretentious and meandering by critics, was his final studio album for 12 years? It shares its name with an unrelated gaming franchise, with both video game and tabletop iterations.

Click here to reveal answer
CYPERPUNK

11.  While the first four albums by this band are undeniably influential classics on a rock and punk culture, their sixth album, Cut the Crap, was thought to be crap by many of their fans, and was their final release. Who are they?

Click here to reveal answer
THE CLASH

12.  While their early career is beloved by both the underground and mainstream, Metallica are known for not one, but two albums that are considered terrible by many, and by some to be among the worst music ever recorded. One is a 2003 album with an accompanying documentary film, and the other is a 2011 collaboration with a highly influential non-metal musician. Name both albums.

Smith's Blurb

Click to view blurb
Click to hide blurb
Welcome to the one day where we celebrate clunkers, noble failures, and misadvised ventures into new creative territory! This will be a one day on established and popular musical acts and the much less successful albums in their discography. Questions should range from ModKos PG-R. There should be something gettable here for fans of most musical styles and time periods. And no, nothing here on Metal Machine Music. That transcends good or bad.
