October 30, 2025  •  LL One-Day Special: League of Legends

Smith's Blurb

League of Legends, produced by Riot Games, is the world's largest esport with over 100 million monthly players. The game puts players into teams of five fighting to destroy the Nexus located deep in the enemy team's base. To do this, players choose from over 150 characters with unique abilities, strengths and weaknesses. Throughout a game (typically lasting 20-40 minutes), you fight NPC "minions", hunt monsters, gain gold to buy powerful items, face off with your opponent, group up for epic 5v5 battles, and push down the map to siege the enemy base and claim victory. Seem like a lot? If you ask me, that's the point. The amount of game knowledge, strategy, and mechanical skills needed to do well has always been what I liked most about the game. There's always something you can improve on - you might say high-level players constitute their own Learned League! (Sorry... that was the best I could come up with.)

I must admit I'm a bit of a fair-weather League of Legends player. I played almost every day when I first encountered the game back in 2013, petered out in 2015, then didn't touch it until 2019, when a college friend lured me back in. Nowadays, when I have a stint of playing it again, I joke to my friends that I've "relapsed" (currently three months clean!). But on serious reflection no game has had a bigger impact on my life. Even given my years off of League, I'm not sure I have put more time into any other game, and when I'm not playing I'm still tuned into its goings-on. It's turned several of my online friends into IRL friends, our bond tempered by grueling hours battling it out on Summoner's Rift. Consider these twelve tidbits of knowledge I've picked up over 12 years as a love letter to the game.

This 1DS will cover League of Legends lore, esports, game history, mechanics, and related media. Obviously, if you have played the game, you will probably be able to get most of these. There are a few that non-players will be able to get - either by consuming other media related to the game, or because of context clues in the questions. Good luck, and remember to Press D to Dance!
